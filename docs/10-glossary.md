# Glossary

[← Back to Index](./README.md) | [← Previous: Using the App](./09-using-the-app.md)

---

Quick reference for all terms used in CAH Scheduler. Terms are organized alphabetically.

---

## A

### Acuity
How sick or complex the patients are. Higher acuity = sicker patients = more staff needed. In CAH Scheduler, acuity and census tier are unified — the nurse manager selects a color tier on the **Census page** each shift, which determines the full staffing requirement.

### ACLS (Advanced Cardiac Life Support)
A certification for handling cardiac emergencies. Required for some assignments.

### Agency Staff
External/contract nurses hired through staffing agencies. Most expensive option. Used as last resort.

### Assignment
A record that connects a staff member to a specific shift. "Maria is assigned to Tuesday Day Shift."

### Audit Trail
Complete history of all changes made in the system. Who changed what, when.

---

## B

### BLS (Basic Life Support)
Standard CPR certification. Usually required for all nursing staff.

---

## C

### Callout
When a scheduled staff member cannot come to work (sick, emergency, etc.).

### CAH (Critical Access Hospital)
A small hospital (≤25 beds) in a rural area, at least 35 miles from another hospital. Has special regulations and funding.

### Census
The number of patients currently in a unit or hospital.

### Census Band
The staffing specification for one census tier. Each unit has four bands — one per color tier — defining exactly how many RNs, LPNs, CNAs, and charge nurses are required. The nurse manager selects a tier on the **Census page**; the system looks up the matching band and enforces its staffing requirements.

### Census Tier
One of four color-coded occupancy levels the nurse manager sets each shift on the **Census page**:
- 🔵 **Blue** — Low occupancy. Low census protocol; some staff may be sent home.
- 🟢 **Green** — Normal census. Standard staffing applies.
- 🟡 **Yellow** — Elevated census. Extra staff need to be called in.
- 🔴 **Red** — Critical census. All hands on deck.

Selecting a tier updates the shift's minimum staffing requirement to that tier's census band specification.

### Charge Nurse
The nurse in charge of a shift. Makes assignments, handles problems, coordinates with doctors.

### Competency Level
A 1-5 rating of a nurse's skill and experience in a specific unit:
- **Level 1**: Novice/Orientee (needs supervision)
- **Level 2**: Advanced Beginner
- **Level 3**: Competent (standard)
- **Level 4**: Proficient (can supervise others)
- **Level 5**: Expert (can be charge, can train)

### CNA (Certified Nursing Assistant)
Staff who provide basic patient care (bathing, feeding, vital signs) under nurse supervision.

### CNO (Chief Nursing Officer)
The top nursing leader in a hospital. Makes policy decisions, approves exceptions.

### Cross-Trained
When a staff member is qualified to work in multiple units. "Maria is cross-trained for ER and ICU."

---

## D

### Day Shift
The daytime work period, typically 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM.

---

## E

### ER (Emergency Room)
The unit that receives emergency patients. Also called ED (Emergency Department).

### Escalation Sequence
The order in which to seek replacement staff when someone calls out: Float → PRN → Overtime → Agency.

---

## F

### Float / Floating
When staff works in a unit other than their home unit. "Maria floated from ICU to ER today."

### Float Pool
Staff members who don't have a home unit - they work wherever needed.

### FTE (Full-Time Equivalent)
A measure of expected work hours:
- 1.0 FTE = 40 hours/week (full-time)
- 0.5 FTE = 20 hours/week (half-time)
- 0.0 FTE = PRN (no guaranteed hours)

---

## H

### Hard Rule
A scheduling rule that cannot be violated. Example: "10 hours minimum rest between shifts."

### Holiday
A special day (Christmas, Thanksgiving, etc.) that affects scheduling and fairness calculations.

### Home Unit
A staff member's primary assigned unit. Where they normally work.

---

## I

### ICU (Intensive Care Unit)
The unit for the most critically ill patients. Requires highest level of care and monitoring.

---

## L

### Leave
Time off from work. Types include vacation, sick, medical, maternity, etc.

### Licensed Staff
RNs and LPNs - staff licensed to perform nursing care. Used in ratio calculations.

### Low Census
When patient count drops below expected levels. Represented by the 🔵 **Blue** census tier. May result in staff being sent home following the unit's low census order (Voluntary → OT → PRN → Full-Time).

### LPN (Licensed Practical Nurse)
A nurse with less training than an RN. Works under RN supervision. Can do most nursing tasks except certain medications.

---

## M

### Med-Surg (Medical-Surgical)
A general care unit for stable patients who don't need intensive care.

---

## N

### Night Shift
The overnight work period, typically 7:00 PM to 7:00 AM.

### No-Show
When staff doesn't appear for their shift and doesn't call in advance.

---

## O

### On-Call
A type of shift where staff isn't at the hospital but must be available to come in if needed.

### Open Shift
A shift that needs coverage, created when leave is approved in advance. Less urgent than a callout. Staff can view and claim open shifts on the Open Shifts page.

### Orientee
A new hire still in training. Level 1 competency. Needs supervision.

### Overtime (OT)
Hours worked beyond 40 per week. Usually paid at 1.5x rate. Expensive to the hospital.

---

## P

### PALS (Pediatric Advanced Life Support)
A certification for handling pediatric emergencies.

### Patient-to-Nurse Ratio
How many patients one nurse is responsible for. Example: "2:1 ratio" = 2 patients per nurse.

### Preceptor
An experienced nurse (Level 5) who trains and supervises new hires.

### PRN (Pro Re Nata)
Latin for "as needed." Staff who work when available, with no guaranteed hours. Also called "per diem."

### Publish (Schedule)
Making the schedule visible to staff. Before publishing, it's a draft.

---

## R

### Reliability Rating
A 1-5 rating of how reliably a staff member shows up for shifts.

### Rest Period
The minimum time between shifts. Usually 10 hours. A hard rule.

### RN (Registered Nurse)
A fully licensed nurse who can perform all nursing duties, including medications.

### Rotation
Taking turns with undesirable duties. "Weekend rotation" = everyone takes turns working weekends.

---

## S

### Safe Harbor
A Texas law allowing nurses to accept an assignment "under protest" if they feel it's unsafe. Provides legal protection.

### Scenario
A possible version of a schedule, with different trade-offs. Managers compare scenarios to choose the best.

### Schedule Period
The time span covered by one schedule. Typically 6 weeks.

### Shift
A specific time period of work. Example: "Day Shift, February 15th."

### Shift Swap
When two staff members trade shifts with each other.

### Sitter
A staff member (usually CNA) assigned to watch one patient continuously (1:1 observation).

### Soft Rule
A scheduling preference that the system tries to honor but can violate if necessary. Example: "Minimize overtime."

### Staffing
Having the right number and type of staff for patient care needs.

---

## T

### TNCC (Trauma Nursing Core Course)
A certification for handling trauma patients. Required for Level 4 competency.

---

## U

### Unit
A distinct area of the hospital with specific patients and staff. Example: ICU, ER, Med-Surg.

---

## V

### VTO (Voluntary Time Off)
When staff volunteers to go home during low census. Staff can indicate "Available for VTO" in their profile. These staff are sent home first when census drops.

---

## W

### Weekend
Saturday and Sunday. Special tracking for fair distribution.

### Weekend Exempt
A flag indicating someone doesn't have to work weekends (for ADA accommodation, religious reasons, etc.).

---

## Quick Reference: Competency Levels

| Level | Name | Can Work Alone? | Can Supervise? | Can Charge? |
|-------|------|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| 1 | Novice | No | No | No |
| 2 | Advanced Beginner | Limited | No | No |
| 3 | Competent | Yes | No | No |
| 4 | Proficient | Yes | Level 2 | No |
| 5 | Expert | Yes | All levels | Yes |

## Quick Reference: Employment Types

| Type | Guaranteed Hours | Cost | Flexibility |
|------|------------------|------|-------------|
| Full-Time | Yes (40/week) | Standard | Low |
| Part-Time | Yes (< 40/week) | Standard | Medium |
| PRN | No | Higher hourly | High |
| Float | Varies | Standard | High |
| Agency | No | Highest | High |

## Quick Reference: Shift Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Assigned | Staff is scheduled |
| Confirmed | Staff acknowledged |
| Called Out | Staff cannot work |
| Swapped | Traded with another |
| Cancelled | No longer needed |
| Flexed | Sent home early |
